About Us

Welcome to Westminster Boating Base, the premier destination for powerboat tuition in central London.

Westminster Boating Base (registered charity number 299412) is an independent charity specialising in powerboating training for both young people and adults. As an accredited training center with the Royal Yachting Association, we offer expert instruction on the River Thames, ensuring a top-tier experience. Nestled in the beautiful Pimlico Gardens, our state-of-the-art facility features spacious function rooms with stunning riverfront balconies, available for corporate and private events. Revenue from these exclusive venue hires directly supports our charitable mission.

What is the RYA Level 2 Award?

Level 2 Powerboat Handling Provides the skills and background knowledge needed by the competent powerboat driver and is the basis of the International Certificate of Competence.

It aims to teach boat handling and seamanship in powerboats. It focuses on low speed close quarters handling, man overboard recovery, an introduction to driving at planing speed, and collision regulations. The course can be taken at an inland or coastal location.

What is the RYA Level 1 and 2

Embark on a comprehensive 2-day course spanning 16 hours, navigating the iconic Thames River in London. Renowned nationwide as the gold standard for boat handling, this course forms the foundation for obtaining the International Certificate of Competence (ICC) for vessels up to 10 meters in length—a requisite for navigating European waters.

Tailored for beginners, the curriculum covers essential topics including close-quarters handling, planing maneuvers, man overboard recovery, and collision regulations. With a maximum student-instructor ratio of 3:1, personalized attention ensures optimal learning outcomes. Emphasizing continuous learning and assessment, this course eschews traditional exams, fostering a dynamic and engaging learning environment.

Course Structure

While participants as young as 10 years old are welcome, RYA Level 2 certification is exclusively attainable for those aged 12 and above.

Throughout the program, students receive ongoing instruction and evaluation to meet RYA standards, culminating in the coveted RYA Powerboat Level 2 certificate upon successful completion. Our dedicated instructors prioritize your enjoyment and success, offering additional hourly tuition when needed—your first hour is complimentary—ensuring your mastery of essential boating skills.

Additional Qualifications (optional)

CEVNI (Code Europeen Des Voies De Navigation Interieure) Test

This enables legal navigation of inland waterways in many European countries and tests your knowledge of signs, lights and symbols that are commonly used. Its a 30 question multiple-choice paper with a pass-mark set at a minimum of 22 correct answers. You will need to be well read on the signage before taking the test. If you are booking a RYA Level 2 Powerboat course and want to take the CEVNI test at the same time, we will make time for it during the course - just let your instructors know at the start of the course. You will need to know your waterway signs, symbols and lights and we advise that you purchase the RYA CEVNI Handbook to aid with your revision.

ICC: International Certificate of Competence (pleasure craft)

Experience smooth sailing with our International Certificate of Competence (ICC) for pleasure craft—a widely recognized certification often likened to a driving license for boats. After completing one of our RYA Powerboat Level 2 courses, you'll be endorsed to command powerboats up to 10 meters in length, without any engine size restrictions. While it's advisable to verify specific requirements with local maritime authorities, rest assured that our certification opens doors to smooth navigation throughout much of Europe.

Issued by the RYA on behalf of the UK Government and authorized by the UK Maritime and Coastguard Agency (MCA), our ICC adheres to Resolution No. 40 of the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UNECE) Working Party on Inland Water Transport. The ICC serves as a passport for leisurely voyages across Europe, granting access to British-flagged vessels and potentially native-flagged vessels, subject to confirmation by local hirers.

Obtaining the ICC is hassle-free, with an administration fee of £50 payable to the RYA upon application, and it's complimentary for RYA members.
